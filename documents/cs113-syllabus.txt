CS113: Theory and Applications of Linear Algebra
Spring 2026
Course Description
Learn the foundational techniques from linear algebra that are essential for mastering the computational
sciences. This course develops the tools necessary for the analysis of linear systems, emphasizing both
abstract notions such as vector spaces, linear maps between them, and their matrix representations, plus
concrete applications such as Markov chains and graphical network analysis. Build proficiency with these
tools by exploring Page Rank, least-squares fitting, and traffic modeling. In addition to mastering
foundational computational tools, this course places an emphasis on using deductive reasoning to
effectively establish the validity of results.
This course requires an online meeting with the instructor outside of normal class hours as part of the final
project. This meeting will be scheduled during weeks 14 or 15.

Unit 2: Vector Space Structure
This unit is about an abstract model of vectors known as a vector space, an example of which is the
solution set to a homogeneous linear system-as seen in Unit 1. These spaces have a special structure.
This structure provides a framework for finding deep connections between applications of interest. The
unit starts with the study of a second familiar example, vectors in Euclidean spaces, before moving to
more complex material.

Unit 3: Linear Transformations
This unit is about maps between vector spaces. Such maps are intimately connected to matrix
multiplication. Two of the many things this allows us to do are changing problem domains to a well-
understood one, and changing coordinates to simplify calculation and/or presentation.

**Preclass Work **is an essential component of this course. Completing the exercises gives you the
opportunity to practice the basic concepts you learn from the videos. In many cases, there are answer
checkers to give you immediate feedback on computations. You will frequently be assessed on the
thoroughness and quality of your answers and given brief formative feedback by the teaching assistant.
If you are absent from CS113:
The content and skills in this course are scaffolded, each session building upon the previous ones.
Missing a class session in CS113 may create a domino effect, as you might find it difficult to follow future
sessions unless you perform the following actions:
1) Watch the pre-class work videos for the missed session,
2) Complete the pre-class work problems, AND
3) Watch the session recording.
Ideally, you should also attempt the breakout problems, as they are crucial aspects of this course.**
**They should be attempted prior to watching the debrief in the video.
You must SUBMIT your answers to the Preclass Work within 1 week of the class session. Failing to do
so results in a 0 on an LO related to the course session.
Makeup Work will take the format of your completed PCW workbook. Submit the link HERE.
