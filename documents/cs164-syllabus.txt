CS164: Optimization Methods
Fall 2025
Course Description
Learn to use and analyze optimization techniques such as linear, quadratic, semidefinite and mixed-integer programming.
Explore optimization algorithms such as Newton’s method, interior point methods and branch and bound methods. Apply these
techniques to robotics, portfolio optimization, physical modeling, and more.
Note: Syllabus subject to change.

Prerequisites & Working Knowledge
The content covered in CS111 and CS113 is assumed knowledge. In particular, students will be expected to have familiarity
with linear algebra and multivariable calculus.
There are four particular things you should review carefully before taking the course:
The limit definition of derivative and its interpretation along with how it relates to linear and quadratic approximation in the
univariate case.
The calculation and interpretation of multivariate gradients, directional derivatives, and their relationship.
The calculation of determinants via minors.
The calculation and interpretation of eigenvalues.
Having a solid grasp of these topics is essential.

Unit 3: Constrained Convex Optimization
Most optimization problems have constraints of some sort imposed on the variables: in economics problems, we might be
constrained to not spend more than we have as income, a road may not be able to support more than a certain number of cars
in a network optimization problem, to mention a couple of examples. To solve constrained optimization problems we’ll have to
delve into the intricacies of convex sets and convex functions. This study culminates in the theorems of Lagrange for equality
type constraints and that of Karush, Kuhn and Tucker for inequality type constraints. We’ll then apply these techniques to
Linear, Quadratic as well as Semidefinite programming problems.
