CS111: Single and Multivariable Calculus
Spring 2026
Course Description
In this course, students learn the principles of single and multivariable calculus needed to succeed in CS
concentration courses and beyond. While a traditional course in these topics focuses on the analytic
techniques required to do complex computations by hand and evaluates students primarily on their
ability to do so, this course takes a different approach. Students learn to understand and apply concepts
to solve problems in various practical contexts. While the standard computational techniques are
covered and practiced, students will fully utilize technologies such as SageMath to supplement their
skills. Students practice communicating and collaborating effectively in quantitative fields throughout the
course.

Unit 2: Differentiation and its Application
Differential calculus is fundamentally about how functions change - often, but not exclusively, with
respect to time. In this unit, such change is studied from the point of view of both a single independent
variable, and multiple independent variables. The ultimate goal is to build competence in solving a large
variety of rate and optimization problems.

Unit 3: Integration and its Application
While integration is not as easy to colloquially define as differentiation, at its heart it is about summation.
It is also a much less compact topic in that there are many more ad hoc techniques, and also general
approaches to problem solving. This unit starts with the essentials of single variable intervals over
intervals and multivariable integrals over rectangular domains. However, it culminates with an exploration
of improper integrals with the goal of understanding, manipulating, and applying distribution functions -
essential tools for probability and statistics.

Pre-class work:
Pre-class work is required for every session in CS111. Class grading will often be done by assessing
completeness and effort for pre-class work. If you find that you struggle to complete some task in the
pre-class work, you are expected to leave a comment describing the issues you face in as much detail
as possible to allow the instructor and TAs to help you as much as possible. You are also expected to
raise any problems you had in completing the pre-class work during the initial discussion of each
session.
If you are likely to be absent from a session, we still expect you to submit your pre-class work for that
session in time.
If you are absent from CS111:
The content and skills in this course are scaffolded, each session building upon the previous ones.
Missing a class session in CS111 may create a domino effect, as you might find it difficult to follow future
sessions unless you perform the following actions:
1) Watch the pre-class work videos for the missed session,
2) Complete the pre-class work problems, AND
3) Watch the session recording.


Ideally, you should also attempt the breakout problems, as they are crucial aspects of this course.**
**They should be attempted prior to watching the debrief in the video. You will not be required to turn in a
report on this make-up work.
